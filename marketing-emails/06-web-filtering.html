If your employees use a computer that's connected to the internet (so, all of them), web filtering should be in place. Full stop.

Here's why. Most cyberattacks don't start with a hacker breaking through your firewall. They start with someone clicking a link. A phishing email that leads to a fake login page. A Google search that lands on a compromised website. An ad that redirects to a malware download. It happens constantly, and it happens to smart people.

Web filtering blocks access to known malicious sites, phishing pages, and categories of websites that have no business being accessed from a work computer. It runs quietly in the background. Your team doesn't notice it until it stops them from making a mistake.

This is considered a baseline security control at this point. Insurance carriers expect it. Compliance frameworks include it. And it's one of the simplest, most cost effective ways to reduce your attack surface.

We deploy web filtering across every device we manage. It works whether your team is in the office or working from home. And it updates automatically as new threats are identified, so you're not relying on a list that was current six months ago.
