Quick heads up on something most people don't know about.

Microsoft added a feature to Windows called Recall. It takes a screenshot of your screen every few seconds and saves it to your computer. The idea is you can "search" through everything you've done on your PC later.

Think about what's on your screen throughout the day. Client emails. Banking logins. Tax documents. Proposals with pricing. Internal conversations. All of that gets captured and stored in a searchable database sitting on the hard drive.

Now imagine that laptop gets stolen. Or someone gets in through a phishing email. That database is the first thing they'd go after, and it's got everything.

Microsoft actually paused the rollout last year when security researchers showed how easy it was to pull that data off a compromised machine. Then they brought it back anyway.

For any business handling sensitive information, whether that's client financials, health records, or government contract data, this is a real problem. It can create compliance headaches under HIPAA, IRS safeguard rules, and CMMC before you even know the feature is turned on.

Most of the businesses we talk to have never heard of it.
