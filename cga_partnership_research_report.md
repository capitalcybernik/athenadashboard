# Cyber Grants Alliance Partnership Research Report
## 10 Target Organizations for Partnership Opportunities

**Report Date:** February 14, 2026  
**Focus:** Organizations serving government contractors, DIB companies, and small businesses needing CMMC compliance

---

## Executive Summary

This report identifies 10 high-potential partnership organizations for Cyber Grants Alliance (CGA). These organizations serve government contractors, defense industrial base (DIB) companies, and small businesses that need cybersecurity compliance (particularly CMMC). Each organization represents a gateway to hundreds or thousands of businesses that could benefit from CGA grant programs.

---

## Ranked Partnership Targets

### 1. **APEX Accelerators (Formerly PTACs)**
- **Website:** https://www.apexaccelerators.org/
- **Mission:** APEX Accelerators (formerly Procurement Technical Assistance Centers) provide counseling and training to help small businesses compete for government contracts. They are the primary resource for businesses entering the federal contracting marketplace.
- **Target Audience:** Small businesses seeking federal, state, and local government contracts; particularly manufacturers, service providers, and technology companies new to government contracting.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** Their member businesses desperately need CMMC compliance to maintain/bid on DoD contracts. Grants through CGA would help their clients afford necessary cybersecurity improvements, increasing contract win rates.
- **Decision Maker:** Executive Director (varies by regional center)
- **Contact:** https://www.apexaccelerators.org/find-your-apex-accelerator/ (find local centers)

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★★**  
*National network with 90+ local centers. Direct access to small businesses entering defense contracting who need initial CMMC compliance support.*

---

### 2. **America's Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs)**
- **Website:** https://www.asbdc.us/
- **Mission:** SBDCs provide free consulting and low-cost training to small businesses across the U.S. With 63 lead centers and 900+ service locations, they're the SBA's largest small business assistance program.
- **Target Audience:** All small businesses, with growing focus on government contractors, cybersecurity, and technology companies.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** SBDC clients increasingly need cybersecurity for federal contracts and general business resilience. CGA grants would be a valuable resource addition to their service portfolio.
- **Decision Maker:** Charles "Tee" Rowe, President & CEO (National Association)
- **Contact:** https://www.asbdc.us/about-us/contact-us/

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★★**  
*Massive national reach. Strong credibility with SBA backing. Already providing cybersecurity counseling - grants would complement their services.*

---

### 3. **National Defense Industrial Association (NDIA)**
- **Website:** https://www.ndia.org/
- **Mission:** NDIA is America's leading defense industry association, promoting national security through strong industrial base partnerships. They connect government, military, and industry stakeholders.
- **Target Audience:** Defense contractors (large and small), government agencies, military leaders, and DIB supply chain companies.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** NDIA members are directly affected by CMMC requirements. Small/mid-tier members especially need grant funding to meet compliance. CGA aligns with NDIA's mission to strengthen the industrial base.
- **Decision Maker:** Gen. (Ret.) Arnold L. Punaro, Chairman; or Membership Director
- **Contact:** membership@ndia.org; https://www.ndia.org/about/contact-us

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★★**  
*Premier defense industry organization. Direct access to DIB companies facing CMMC mandates. High credibility in defense sector.*

---

### 4. **Professional Services Council (PSC)**
- **Website:** https://www.pscouncil.org/
- **Mission:** PSC is the leading trade association for government contractors in the technology and professional services sectors. They advocate for federal contractors and provide member resources.
- **Target Audience:** Government contractors providing technology, professional services, consulting, and IT solutions to federal agencies.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** PSC members are heavily affected by cybersecurity requirements including CMMC. Small and mid-size members need financial assistance for compliance.
- **Decision Maker:** David J. Berteau, President & CEO
- **Contact:** info@pscouncil.org; https://www.pscouncil.org/contact/

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★☆**  
*Strong presence in DC/Virginia/Maryland area. Represents contractors heavily affected by CMMC. Strong advocacy voice.*

---

### 5. **Virginia Small Business Development Center (VSBDC)**
- **Website:** https://www.virginiasbdc.org/
- **Mission:** The Virginia SBDC provides business counseling, training, and resources to Virginia's small businesses, with special programs for government contractors given Virginia's massive defense presence.
- **Target Audience:** Virginia-based small businesses, especially those in Northern Virginia, Hampton Roads, and Richmond defense corridors seeking government contracts.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** Virginia has the highest concentration of defense contractors per capita. VSBDC clients urgently need CMMC compliance grants to maintain Pentagon contracts.
- **Decision Maker:** David T. Webster, State Director
- **Contact:** https://www.virginiasbdc.org/contact/

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★★**  
*Top priority state for CMMC given Pentagon proximity. High concentration of cleared contractors. Strong state-level programs.*

---

### 6. **Maryland Small Business Development Center (MDSBDC)**
- **Website:** https://www.mdsbdc.org/
- **Mission:** MDSBDC provides comprehensive business assistance to Maryland's small businesses, with specialized programs for government contractors and cybersecurity/technology firms.
- **Target Audience:** Maryland small businesses, particularly those near Fort Meade, Aberdeen Proving Ground, and NSA seeking federal contracts.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** Maryland's defense and intelligence community presence makes CMMC critical for local businesses. CGA grants would be highly relevant to MDSBDC clients.
- **Decision Maker:** Kathleen M. Murphy, State Director
- **Contact:** https://www.mdsbdc.org/contact-us

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★☆**  
*Major defense/intel hub state. Cyber corridor from Baltimore to DC. Strong tech startup ecosystem needing compliance support.*

---

### 7. **Association of the United States Army (AUSA)**
- **Website:** https://www.ausa.org/
- **Mission:** AUSA is the Army's premier professional association, supporting soldiers, their families, and the industrial base that supplies the Army. They connect industry with Army requirements.
- **Target Audience:** Army contractors, suppliers, service providers, and small businesses in the Army industrial base.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** AUSA members include thousands of small businesses supplying the Army. CMMC compliance is mandatory for most Army contracts - grants would help their small business members.
- **Decision Maker:** Gen. (Ret.) Robert Brooks, President; or Director of Membership
- **Contact:** https://www.ausa.org/contact

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★☆**  
*Massive Army contractor network. Strong small business council. Regular events for industry-government connection.*

---

### 8. **National Defense Transportation Association (NDTA)**
- **Website:** https://www.ndtahq.org/
- **Mission:** NDTA supports the defense transportation and logistics community, connecting government, military, and commercial transportation providers who support national security.
- **Target Audience:** Defense logistics providers, transportation companies, supply chain firms, and government contractors in the movement/transport sector.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** Transportation/logistics companies in the defense supply chain are often overlooked for CMMC but need compliance. CGA grants would help them secure DoD logistics contracts.
- **Decision Maker:** Vice Admiral (Ret.) Al Konetzni, President & CEO
- **Contact:** hq@ndtahq.org; https://www.ndtahq.org/contact

**Partnership Potential: ★★★☆☆**  
*Critical but under-served sector. Logistics companies often lack cybersecurity resources. Strong government partnerships already in place.*

---

### 9. **Veterans Business Development Council (VBDC) / National Veteran Business Development Council (NVBDC)**
- **Website:** https://nvbdc.org/
- **Mission:** NVBDC certifies veteran-owned businesses and provides resources to help them compete in the federal marketplace. They work extensively with VA and DoD contractors.
- **Target Audience:** Veteran-owned small businesses (VOSBs), service-disabled veteran-owned small businesses (SDVOSBs), and their supply chain partners.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** Veteran-owned businesses receive federal contracting preferences but often lack cybersecurity resources for CMMC compliance. CGA grants would be a powerful tool for this community.
- **Decision Maker:** Keith King, President & CEO
- **Contact:** info@nvbdc.org; https://nvbdc.org/contact/

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★☆**  
*Large veteran business community. Strong federal contracting focus. Under-served for cybersecurity resources. Mission-aligned with supporting veterans.*

---

### 10. **Northern Virginia Chamber of Commerce (Nova Chamber)**
- **Website:** https://www.novachamber.org/
- **Mission:** The Northern Virginia Chamber serves the business community in the DC suburbs, representing the largest concentration of government contractors and defense companies outside the Pentagon.
- **Target Audience:** Northern Virginia businesses, particularly defense contractors, government service providers, cybersecurity firms, and technology companies.
- **Why Partner with CGA:** This chamber represents ground zero for CMMC impact. Their members are predominantly government contractors who need immediate compliance assistance.
- **Decision Maker:** Julie Coons, President & CEO
- **Contact:** info@novachamber.org; https://www.novachamber.org/contact

**Partnership Potential: ★★★★★**  
*Highest concentration of affected businesses. Direct access to defense contractors. Strong programming already in place for government contracting.*

## Partnership Approach Strategy

### Priority 1: National Networks (APEX, SBDC, NDIA)
- Approach national headquarters first
- Emphasize turnkey grant opportunity for their members
- Offer co-branded webinars and resource materials

### Priority 2: Regional Powerhouses (Virginia, Maryland SBDCs)
- Leverage CMMC urgency in high-impact regions
- Focus on immediate contract retention concerns
- Partner on in-person events and workshops

### Priority 3: Industry Associations (PSC, AUSA, NDTA)
- Position as member benefit enhancement
- Integrate with existing conferences and events
- Co-develop educational content about cybersecurity grants

### Priority 4: Specialized Communities (NVBDC, NOVA Chamber)
- Tailor messaging to specific community needs
- Highlight competitive advantage grants provide
- Build grassroots advocacy for CGA programs

---

## Recommended Outreach Sequence

1. **Week 1-2:** Contact APEX Accelerators national office and Virginia/Maryland SBDC directors
2. **Week 3-4:** Reach out to NDIA and PSC membership departments
3. **Week 5-6:** Engage AUSA and NVBDC leadership
4. **Week 7-8:** Contact NDTA and NOVA Chamber

---

## Key Messaging Points for Outreach

1. **Immediate Pain Point:** CMMC compliance is now a contract requirement, not optional
2. **Financial Barrier:** Small businesses need help affancing cybersecurity upgrades
3. **Competitive Advantage:** Grant recipients can meet requirements competitors cannot
4. **No Cost to Partner:** CGA grants are free resources they can offer members
5. **Member Retention:** Help members keep existing contracts and win new ones

These 10 organizations represent direct pathways to thousands of government contractors and DIB companies that need CMMC compliance support. The combination of national reach (APEX, SBDC, NDIA), regional density (Virginia/Maryland SBDCs), and specialized communities (NVBDC, NOVA Chamber) creates a comprehensive partnership strategy.

The urgency of CMMC implementation makes this an ideal time for Cyber Grants Alliance outreach - these organizations are actively seeking resources to help their members comply, and CGA grants fill a critical gap in the ecosystem.
