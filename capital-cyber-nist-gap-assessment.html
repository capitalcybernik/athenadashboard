WHAT IS A NIST 800-171 GAP ASSESSMENT?

A NIST 800-171 gap assessment is a comprehensive evaluation of your organization's current cybersecurity practices against the 110 security requirements defined in NIST Special Publication 800-171.

The assessment answers three critical questions:

  1. Where are we now? What controls are already implemented?
  2. Where do we need to be? What gaps exist against NIST requirements?
  3. How do we get there? What is the prioritized remediation roadmap?

Why It Matters:

  • Required for accurate SPRS scoring
  • Foundation for CMMC certification
  • Identifies security risks before attackers do
  • Creates defensible compliance documentation
  • Prioritizes investments for maximum impact

FAQs

Q: How long does a gap assessment take?

Standard assessments take 2 weeks from kickoff to final report. Comprehensive assessments take 3 weeks. The actual time commitment from your team is typically 4-6 hours of interviews and evidence gathering.

Q: What's the difference between a gap assessment and a readiness assessment?

A gap assessment identifies where you are vs. where you need to be. A readiness assessment is the final check before your C3PAO assessment to ensure you're actually ready. We recommend a gap assessment early in your journey and a readiness assessment 2-4 weeks before your official CMMC assessment.

Q: Do I need to prepare anything before the assessment?

We'll provide a preparation checklist, but generally you should have:

  • Network diagrams (even rough ones)
  • List of systems and software used
  • Current policies (if any exist)
  • Key personnel available for interviews
  • Access to IT systems for our assessors

Q: Will you fix the gaps you find?

The gap assessment is diagnostic only. However, we offer implementation services to remediate the gaps we identify. Many clients engage us for both the assessment and remediation because we already understand their environment.

Q: How accurate is your SPRS score calculation?

We calculate your SPRS score based on the same methodology used by the DoD. Our assessments typically predict SPRS scores within 5-10 points of official scores. We also identify quick wins to improve your score before submission.
